Login App

Login App

Expand

For a third year university module I created two login apps with different technologies. View the full project at MED6103, along with the MEAN Stack version and the Ruby on Rails version.

The project at large was designed to help us not only create something useful, but also to get us thinking about what we might want to do after we graduate. I took the opportunity to work on some back end stuff as I had never done this before, plus I needed it for a different university project. These login apps are the end result.

The login apps don't have to much going on in the way of design, but that wasn't the focus of this project. The apps have fully working authorisation and authentication systems, along with the ability to update user profiles. They gave me experience with various different technologies such as Node.js and Ruby on Rails, and provide an excellent base for another project I'm working on.

Ultimately, whilst I enjoyed this project and I got something great out of it, it did make me realise that front end development was the direction I want to head in.

Technologies: AngularJS, Node.js, Express, MongoDB, Ruby on Rails, Jade, Stylus.

Teen Talks

Teen Talks

Expand

Teen Talks is an online magazine about teenage life.

The focus of the website is on the content, so the design needs to be simple, allowing the content to take centre stage. The design is simple and clean, thus allowing the content to be the main focus.

The website has several different categories each with its own page, along with a slider showing the latest posts and various widgets. The site uses specific colours and fonts to be in line with the Teen Talks branding. The site is responsive and allows the client to post their content with ease.

Technologies: HTML, CSS, PHP, JavaScript, WordPress.

Music Label Manager

Music Label Manaager

Expand

Music Label Manager is an online game that allows user to create their own record label, competing against other records labels. Points are based on the performance of your signed artists on music services.

Built for my final major university project, the website had to be industry standard and follow industry trends. The website is built upon a MEAN stack login app built previously, and utilises technologies such as sockets, the Last.fm API, and various node modules.

The game allows users to choose a scene for their label, sign artists to their label for a period up to 8 weeks, and compete against the other labels in and out of their scene. Although the game only currently utilises Last.fm data, there are plans to expand the range of APIs used to procide data for scoring, as well as features such as the ability to buy out contracts and poach artists from other labels.

Technologies: AngularJS, Node.js, Express, MongoDB, Jade, Stylus, Sockets.io, Last.fm API.

Online Artist Tracker

Online Artist Tracker.

Expand

Built for a university project, the Online Artist Tracker uses several music service APIs to look up and keep track of an artist's, album's or song's stats.

Users can select a service from Soundcloud, Last.fm, or This Is My Jam, search for their desired artist, album, or song and then will be shown stats such as listeners and plays from the service they selected.

Technologies: HTML, CSS, JavaScript, jQuery, Soundcloud, Last.fm, This Is My Jam APIs.
